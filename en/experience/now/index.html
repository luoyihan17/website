I joined Tencent Music Entertainment's QQ Music design team in 2024, focusing on product experience design for AI music creation, AI singing, smart terminals, and innovative music interaction experiences. Since joining, I have been deeply involved in productizing QQ Music's AI creation capabilities, continuously optimizing AI song generation, AI singing, AI arrangement, AIMV, and other core experiences around the goal of lowering the barrier to music creation and enabling everyday users to participate in making music.

In AI music creation, I participated in and led the design of features including one-sentence song generation, conversational song creation, humming-to-song, image-to-song, music recipes, and AI generation process visualization. My work focused on reducing input cost, clarifying creation paths, improving waiting experiences, and increasing users' sense of control over results. These designs helped transform AI creation from a technical capability demo into a music experience that is easier to understand, more participatory, and more shareable, supporting growth in both user activity and business revenue.

I also worked on smart terminal and cross-device experiences such as QQ Music TV, HarmonyOS, and Xiaomi Lite. Around listening behaviors in different device contexts, I designed player upgrades, lock-screen lyrics, status-bar lyrics, transliterated lyrics, healing mode, and karaoke entry optimizations, improving QQ Music's consistency and contextual expression across the multi-device ecosystem.

Since 2025, I have further explored the combination of Vibe Coding, AI prototyping tools, and gamified music interaction. I rapidly built prototypes such as an interactive playlist gacha machine, Mood Slot Machine, music parkour, album-cover match game, 3D Polaroid player, and 360-degree music space. These explorations extend music experiences beyond traditional playback tools into containers for games, social interaction, emotional expression, and personalized creation.

Overall, my work at QQ Music focuses on translating complex technical capabilities in AI music creation and cross-device music experiences into product experiences that users can understand, participate in, and share. I also continue to explore how designers in the AI era can move from interface designers toward experience definers and prototype producers.
