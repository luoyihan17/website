I earned my B.S. in Interaction Design from ArtCenter College of Design. ArtCenter's training emphasized intensive project practice, clear communication, systematic interaction thinking, and turning ideas quickly into design proposals that can be discussed, tested, and refined.

During my studies, I was on the Dean's List (GPA > 3.8/4.0), received long-term scholarships, and graduated with honors.

This experience became a foundation for my later work in AI product design, VR UX, interactive media research, and cultural curation: paying attention to experience details while evaluating design through real users, product goals, and business contexts.

Core takeaways:

  • Interaction design methods and UX thinking;
  • Prototyping, interface design, and experience storytelling;
  • Intensive project-based training and public presentation;
  • Cross-disciplinary design thinking and future experience exploration.
